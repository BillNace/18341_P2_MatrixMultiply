Clock Constraints
We want Vivado to know what your clock is! To do so, you’ll need to edit your constraints file.

Set it up 
Here are some guiding questions to think through:

What is the name of the clock port in your HDL design?
How do you tell Vivado which physical pin a port connects to? (Hint: Look at how other signals are defined in your constraints file. What property sets the pin location?)
Which pin of the Boolean board is the dedicated input for the onboard 100MHz oscillator? What voltage standard does that pin use? 
A clock spends half its period high and half low. If it starts at 0 ns, when does it go high, and when does it go low again?
Once you’ve thought through those, try writing a set_property and create_clock line addresses these factors…

Check your work
Once you’ve added both lines, run Implementation and look at the Worst Negative Slack (WNS) value in the timing summary. If it shows a number rather than “N/A”, Vivado found your clock!
